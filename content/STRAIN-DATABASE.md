## INDICA-DOMINANT STRAINS

### 1. Granddaddy Purple (GDP)

**Genetics:** Purple Urkle x Big Bud
**Type:** Indica-dominant (80% Indica / 20% Sativa)
**THC:** 17-24%
**CBD:** <1%

**Terpene Profile:**
- Myrcene (dominant): 0.8-1.2%

- Pinene: 0.1-0.2%

- Insomnia and sleep disorders
- Muscle spasms and tension
- Appetite stimulation
- Stress and anxiety relief

**Research Notes:**
High myrcene content contributes to sedative effects. Berry anthocyanins provide antioxidant properties.

**Growing Info:**
- Flowering: 8-9 weeks
- Yield: Medium-High
- Difficulty: Intermediate
- Climate: Indoor preferred, 68-80°F
- Notes: Purple coloration enhanced by cooler night temps

**Origin:** California, USA (Ken Estes, 2003)

## TERPENE REFERENCE GUIDE

### Myrcene
- **Aroma:** Earthy, musky, fruity (mango)
- **Effects:** Sedating, muscle relaxant, anti-inflammatory
- **Boiling Point:** 168°C (334°F)
- **Also Found In:** Mango, hops, thyme, lemongrass
- **Medical Uses:** Pain, insomnia, inflammation
- **Note:** >0.5% myrcene typically indicates sedating effects

### Limonene
- **Aroma:** Citrus, lemon, orange
- **Effects:** Mood elevation, stress relief, anti-anxiety
- **Boiling Point:** 176°C (349°F)
- **Also Found In:** Citrus fruits, juniper, peppermint
- **Medical Uses:** Depression, anxiety, acid reflux
- **Note:** Enhances absorption of other terpenes

### Caryophyllene
- **Aroma:** Spicy, peppery, woody
- **Effects:** Anti-inflammatory, pain relief
- **Boiling Point:** 160°C (320°F)
- **Also Found In:** Black pepper, cloves, cinnamon
- **Medical Uses:** Pain, anxiety, inflammation
- **Note:** Only terpene that binds to CB2 receptors

### Pinene
- **Aroma:** Pine, rosemary, fir
- **Effects:** Alertness, memory retention, bronchodilator
- **Boiling Point:** 155°C (311°F)
- **Also Found In:** Pine needles, rosemary, basil
- **Medical Uses:** Asthma, inflammation, memory
- **Note:** Most common terpene in nature

### Linalool
- **Aroma:** Floral, lavender, spicy
- **Effects:** Calming, anti-anxiety, analgesic
- **Boiling Point:** 198°C (388°F)
- **Also Found In:** Lavender, mint, cinnamon
- **Medical Uses:** Anxiety, depression, insomnia, seizures
- **Note:** Enhances sedative effects of myrcene

### Humulene
- **Aroma:** Hoppy, earthy, woody
- **Effects:** Appetite suppressant, anti-inflammatory
- **Boiling Point:** 106°C (223°F)
- **Also Found In:** Hops, coriander, cloves
- **Medical Uses:** Inflammation, pain, appetite control
- **Note:** One of few appetite-suppressing terpenes

### Terpinolene
- **Aroma:** Floral, herbal, piney
- **Effects:** Uplifting, sedating (paradoxical)
- **Boiling Point:** 186°C (367°F)
- **Also Found In:** Nutmeg, tea tree, apples
- **Medical Uses:** Anxiety, insomnia, antioxidant
- **Note:** Less common, found in Jack Herer genetics

### Ocimene
- **Aroma:** Sweet, herbal, woody
- **Effects:** Decongestant, antiviral
- **Boiling Point:** 100°C (212°F)
- **Also Found In:** Mint, parsley, orchids
- **Medical Uses:** Congestion, viruses, inflammation
- **Note:** Often found in sativas

## LEGAL FRAMEWORKS BY REGION

### Spain
- **Status:** Decriminalized for personal use
- **Cannabis Clubs:** Legal (private consumption)
- **Medical:** Legal but limited access
- **Cultivation:** Legal for personal use (private)
- **Public Consumption:** Illegal (fines)
- **Note:** Grey area - clubs operate in legal ambiguity

### United Kingdom
- **Status:** Illegal (Class B)
- **Medical:** Legal with prescription (Schedule 2)
- **Clinics:** Alternaleaf, Sapphire, Cantourage
- **Cultivation:** Illegal without license
- **CBD:** Legal (<0.2% THC, novel food regulated)
- **Note:** Medical program expanding rapidly

### Netherlands
- **Status:** "Tolerated" (gedoogbeleid)
- **Coffee Shops:** Licensed to sell (5g max)
- **Medical:** Legal with prescription
- **Cultivation:** Illegal (paradox with supply)
- **Note:** Front door legal, back door illegal

### Germany
- **Status:** Medical legal, recreational legalizing
- **Medical:** Comprehensive since 2017
- **Recreational:** Legalization in progress (2024)
- **Cultivation:** Personal growing being legalized
- **Note:** Largest medical market in Europe

### United States
- **Federal:** Illegal (Schedule I)
- **State:** Varies - 24+ states recreational legal
- **Medical:** 38+ states legal
- **Note:** Federal-state conflict ongoing

### Canada
- **Status:** Fully legal (2018)
- **Federal:** Legal recreational and medical
- **Provinces:** Regulate retail and consumption
- **Home Grow:** Up to 4 plants (federal)
- **Note:** Second country to federally legalize

## HARM REDUCTION GUIDELINES

### Synthetic Cannabinoid Warnings
**Signs of Synthetic Contamination:**
- Unnaturally uniform appearance
- Chemical or solvent smell
- Unusually potent effects
- Extremely low price
- Unknown source

**Dangers:**
- Unpredictable potency
- No quality control
- Linked to deaths and hospitalizations
- May cause psychosis, seizures, organ damage

**If Suspected:** DO NOT CONSUME. Dispose safely.

### Quality Red Flags
- **Mold:** White fuzzy patches (not trichomes), musty smell
- **PGR:** Unnaturally dense, brown hairs, few trichomes
- **Pesticides:** Chemical smell, residue
- **Poor Cure:** Too wet, too dry, no smell
- **Seeds:** Indicates hermaphrodite or poor cultivation

### Safe Consumption Guidelines
1. Start low, go slow (especially edibles)
2. Know your source
3. Store properly (dark, cool, airtight)
4. Don't drive under influence
5. Be aware of drug interactions
6. Stay hydrated
7. Have CBD available (can reduce THC anxiety)

### When to Seek Help
- Extreme anxiety or panic
- Rapid heartbeat
- Difficulty breathing
- Paranoia or psychosis symptoms
- Signs of allergic reaction

**Emergency:** Call local emergency services. Cannabis itself is not lethal but can trigger underlying conditions.
