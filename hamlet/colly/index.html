🌟 Why He's Here

Every path that exists today was walked by someone first — often alone, often at personal cost, long before it was safe or legal to walk it publicly. Colly walked that path for decades.

Cannabin-Oid exists to take people from self-medication to prescription. That journey was pioneered by people like Donald Denzler — loudly, defiantly, with a laugh that could fill a room. Every person who gets a legal prescription today walks on ground he helped break.

"We stand on the shoulders of giants."

This is his hamlet. The first honorary hamlet in the village. Not because he asked for it — he never would have. Because he earned it.

🌿 Who Was Colly?

Donald Denzler — known the world over as Dr. Colly Light — was a German-born cannabis advocate, inventor, artist, and philosopher who spent his final years living freely in Gran Canaria. With his long white beard, dreadlocks, and unmistakable presence, he was impossible to ignore. But it was what he said that made him unforgettable.

For over two decades he documented his life on YouTube — nearly 35,000 subscribers, 328 videos — with one guiding motto:

"Wie ich mein Leben lebe."
How I live my life. — @CollyLight_Original · the channel that became a monument

He didn't perform a life. He lived one. He shared health struggles, poverty, joy, anger, his animals, his garden, his philosophy — all of it, unfiltered. In a world of curated feeds, Colly was stubbornly, beautifully real.

🔬 The VapBong — A Legacy in Ceramic

Donald's greatest practical gift to cannabis culture was an invention that arrived decades before the industry caught up with it: the VapBong. A handcrafted ceramic vaporizer he designed himself — combining the ritual of the bong with the clean practice of vaporization. Every component, every firing, every glaze, done by hand.

The artistic realisation came through collaboration with Jaxel's Art and the CannaKunst community — described simply as: "Colly Light: the inventor. Jaxel's Art: the artist."

His son Felix Denzler carries the flame forward through ceramics at Vapotterie.com — a quiet inheritance from his father. The craft continues. The hands remember.
